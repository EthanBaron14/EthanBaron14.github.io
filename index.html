About Me


Hi!

My name is Ethan and I am a fourth-year student pursuing a dual degree, with a Bachelor of Science in Finance and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science.

I attend school during the academic year at The George Washington University in Washington, DC. Accordingly, I am pursuing coursework in the George Washington University School of Business and School of Engineering and Applied Science.

When I'm not at school, I enjoy visiting my family in Greenvale, NY. Only about 40 minutes from New York City, I am fortunate enough to live near two of the best cities in the United States.

I am actively involved extracurricularly in both disciplines that I am studying. Starting in Spring 2020, I accepted a position where I have since served as a member of course instructional staff in the Computer Science department. I have worked on a number of classes, including Database Systems & Team Projects, Operating Systems, Software Engineering, and Alogorithms & Data Structures. Through this opportunity, I have gained experience teaching others complicated information in a way that makes it not only easy for them to learn, but also to apply in their studies.

In Spring 2022, I was appointed by the GW Investment Institute to serve as the Teaching Assistant for the Quantitative Trading course, run through the Finance department. Alongside the Vice Dean of Undergraduate Programs, I revised course learning outcomes, created a new syllabus, and facilitated the creation of new assignments. I also provided support to the students on their semesterlong project and helped with grading assignments.

With the guidance of my mentor, I created template validation features, reducing the number of customer actions required to validate templates by 50%. Through this experience, I mastered Kotlin and nine AWS technologies. I really appreciated the opportunity to gain exposure to new languages and new technologies, all with the support of an amazing team.

When I have some time off, I enjoy going out to eat with my friends, attending electronic music concerts, and exploring DC with my dog, Davie.

Database Systems and Team Projects

One of the largest projects I contributed to for one of my courses, CSCI 2541 - Database Systems and Team Projects provided an interesting challenge that I had not been exposed to before: project integration.

In this class, I worked in a group of three to build one of the modules for a graduate computer science program web platform. My group was tasked with building the application system, complete with a LAMP stack back-end and Bootstrap front-end.

After building and styling the application processing system, my group demonstrated the functionality of the platform to instructional staff. We were then broken up into a different set of groups of three students.

In my second group, composed of students who built different modules of the platform, we were tasked with integrating the modules to form one cohesive system that looked consistent and accessed appropriate data from the MySQL database.

This was a unique challenge because I had not had exposure to project integration. We had to rework the database tables to integrate each of our modules such that they could all function together. We then added analytics queries, statistics, and a set of extra features.

The class culminated in a final demo in front of the instructional staff where our integration and functionality was tested in real-time. Our platform was exposed to rigorous testing and evaluation, including edge-case and bad input testing.
